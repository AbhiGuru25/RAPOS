01  Problem Statement

Retail investors rely on intuition and react slowly to volatile markets, lacking access to quantitative tools that process high-frequency financial data and generate scientifically optimal portfolio decisions in real time.

Research Question

"Can an LSTM deep learning model trained on 60-day OHLCV sequences accurately predict next-day closing prices, and can these predictions drive an automated, risk-adjusted portfolio rebalancing system deployable at production scale?"

03  Methodology
1
Data Ingestion
OHLCV data fetched live from Finnhub API, cleaned, and normalized using MinMaxScaler to [0,1] range.
2
Feature Engineering
10 features computed: RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, SMA 7/14/30, daily volatility, and volume delta.
3
LSTM Training
2-layer LSTM (128→64 units, Dropout 0.2). Adam optimizer, MSE loss, 50 epochs on 80% of data.
4
Risk Score Generation
Predicted returns + Sharpe Ratio + historical volatility combined into a dynamic AI Risk Score (1–10).
5
Portfolio Optimisation
Markowitz MPT applied to LSTM predictions. Output: exact BUY/SELL signals per asset class.
6
Live Deployment
FastAPI backend on Render.com serves real-time inference. Frontend on Vercel with WebSocket sync.

07  Results & Discussion
📊
High Prediction FidelityThe LSTM model achieves 94.2% accuracy on the held-out test set, demonstrating strong generalization to unseen market conditions driven by the 60-day memory cell.
⚖️
Effective Risk ScoringThe AI Risk Score (1–10) accurately reflects true market volatility — high scores correctly preceded observed downturns in TSLA and BTC during testing.
🔄
Real-Time PerformanceWith 24ms inference latency and 10-second portfolio sync via Supabase WebSockets, RAPOS operates at near-institutional data update frequencies.
📐
Markowitz IntegrationCombining LSTM predictions with Modern Portfolio Theory produced optimal BUY/SELL signals that outperformed a static, equal-weight baseline portfolio.
